Kanji

Kanji is one one of the three writing systems used in Japanese. Kanji are adopted Chinese characters and while many of them retain thier original (or similar) meanings, the pronunciations are often different. There are many different kanji lists, some designed by the Japanese governement to for education purposes. To learn more about kanji click here.

We'll be foucsing on JLPT kanji. JLPT stands for Japanese-Language Proficiency Test, this test focuses on reading and listening skills as well as vocabulary and grammar. There are 5 levels, N5 being the easiest and N1 being the hardest.

Want to test you knowledge of kanji?


Photo by: Borrill, 2019


N5

At this level your able to understand some basic Japanese. You should be able to read hiragana, katakana and some basic kanji. To pass this level you'll need to know about 80 to 100 kanji.


N4

At this level your able to understand basic Japanese. You should be able to read and understand simple passages using everyday vocabulary. There are about 170 kanji unique to this level, you'll need to know about 250 kanji total.


N3

At this level your able to understand Japanese that would be used in everyday life. You should be able to read and understand passages about everyday topics. There are about 370 kanji unique to this level, you'll need to know about 650 kanji total.


N2

At this level your able to understand Japanese that would be used in everyday conversation with a wider variety of vocabulary. You should be able to read articles, passeges and newspapers about various toipcs. There are about 370 kanji unique to this level, you'll need to know about 1000 kanji total.


N1

At this level your able to understand Japanese at a near fulent level. You should be able to read and understand passages about everyday topics. Click here to view a list of kanji unique to this level, you'll need to know about 2000 kanji total.
