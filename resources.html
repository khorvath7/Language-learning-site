Resources

There are many great resources for learning Japanese. Many people, beginners especailly, may have trouble finding the textbook that right for them. Differnet textbooks may be better suited to differnt learning styles so picking the one that's right for you can be a challenge. You can search for textbooks using the guide below.

Textbooks are a great way to learn Japanese in a structured way; however, emmersing yourself in the language will be most beneficial. Other ways to learn and improve your Japanese skills includes reading books, watching tv shows and movies, listening to podcasts or watching vlogs. Of course there are many other options for learning and everyone is different so finding what works best for you may take some time.

Book Title Summary Pros Cons Level
Genki One of the most popular textbooks for beginners
  • Workbooks avaliable
  • Grammar points are explained in English
  • Not intended for self study
  • Answer keys sold separetly
 Beginner
Minna No Nihongo Another popular choice for beginners, the textbook has a wide variety of activities.
  • Has reading, writing, speaking and listening activities
  • Introduces kanji from the beginning
  • Not intended for self study
  • Answer keys sold separetly
 Beginner to Intermediate
Guide to Learning Japanese Grammar Great guide for learning the structure of the language, easy for beginners to comprehend.
  • Free online resource
  • Designed for self study
  • No listening excercises
 Beginner to Intermediate
Tobira Used in many upper level Japanese classes, it is a great textbook for intermediate to advanced learners.
  • Has speaking and listening activities
  • Can be used for self study
  • Fast paced, can be overwhelming
 Intermedaite to Advanced
